GRAMMAR RULES

Words are split into two types, male and female. Male typically ending in O, while female ends in A. Some words however, are unchanged and would stay the same, these are usually nouns. Adjectives describing a noun or pronoun come after the word they are describing and would match the gender of the word, taking the A or O suffix. The verb “SER” stands for “to be”, but takes many forms in order to fit with other pronouns.

SER

SER is a situational word that changes form depending on what word precedes it. It is only preceded by pronouns such as they, he, she, and I.
